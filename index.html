What Shaped My Approach

Before I helped brands convert, I learned how to build trust with total strangers. Fast.

I started at a nonprofit, welcoming new donors over the phone.

Built such good rapport that supporters even sent me Valentine's cards.

My boss joked it was unfair, especially since I already had a girlfriend.

Later, I helped run nationwide street fundraising campaigns.

Boosted donations 10–20% a year and cutting donor attrition by 22% through smarter engagement systems.

What I learned?

Trust, timing, and emotional clarity matter more than any tactic.

Then I ran my own fashion brand for several years.

Built it from scratch, showcased at London Fashion Week, sold globally B2B and DTC.

Pioneered influencer collabs before it was a thing.

Became a founding brand in ASOS’s first sustainable fashion concept.

Built a 5,000-person email list... and did absolutely nothing with it.

I know what it’s like to be deep in the weeds...

Juggling logistics, building an audience, and trying to make what you sell resonate.

Now I help brands skip those mistakes.

Today, the same principles — trust, retention, clarity — shape how I write copy that connects, converts, and keeps customers coming back.

When not writing copy, I'm moving, meditating, hiking, or birdwatching (yes, I'm that person).

I grow things. Flowers, vegetables, experiments.

Berlin keeps me sharp. Nature keeps me grounded.

Long-term? Mountains, birds, and helping people reconnect with nature.

For now, I’m here to help your copy work harder.
